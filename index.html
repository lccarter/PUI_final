About

On October 27th, 2018, the unspeakable occurred when a gunman burst into a busy Pittsburgh synagogue and opened fire, killing 11 people and injuring six others. Since then, the communities of Squirrel Hill and Pittsburgh at large have been trying to rebuild and pick up the pieces. In particular, a famous quote by the late Mister Rogers (a former Squirrel Hill resident) has given people hope:

“When I was a boy and I would see scary things in the news, my mother would say to me, "Look for the helpers. You will always find people who are helping.” - Fred Rogers

This project was made to help formulate a restorative narrative surrounding the horrific tragedy in an effort to move forward. "Restorative narrative" is a genre of stories that demonstrate how people and communities are making a meaningful progression from a place of despair to a place of resilience. Through this project, we aim to show how Pittsburgh has demonstrated to the rest of the world how to be a good neighbor by showing examples of "the helpers" throughout the city.
